good questions

a few things people ask

what is BackPocket Mom?

BackPocket Mom is a calm family organizer app, designed to gather the scattered logistics of family life — schedules, reminders, documents, and memories — into one private, trustworthy place. think of it as a warm, wise helper in your pocket: the 'mom' is the app, not you, and it lightens the load without ever judging.

who is it for?

it's built for busy families of every shape: parents juggling work and kids, single parents, co-parents in one home or two, grandparents and caregivers lending a hand, and fur-parents with unpredictable schedules. it's designed to meet every generation where they are, so it never assumes you're already tech-savvy.

what can it help me with?

it's designed to keep one calm family calendar — speak an event or connect the apps you already use — plus a document vault for the important paper, gentle night-before reminders, a plain-words read on how heavy the day feels, and a way to share just what a sitter or grandparent needs.

will it be on iPhone and Android?

yes — BackPocket Mom is designed for both iPhone and Android, with a home-screen widget planned for each so your next event and today's timeline are a glance away. it isn't released yet; join the list and we'll email you the day it's ready on the App Store and Google Play.

how does it protect my family's privacy?

privacy is the heart of it. we will never sell or share your family's data — the app is designed to be funded by subscriptions, not by monetizing you. your records are built to be private and encrypted, and you'll be able to see, export, or delete everything we hold, anytime.

can co-parents in separate homes use it?

yes — that's a core idea. each parent has their own private identity, and a single switch flips between combined mode for one household and separate mode for two. a protected child-safety floor — allergies, current medications, emergency contacts — is designed to stay visible to both, so safety is never hidden.

can a babysitter or grandparent see only what they need?

that's the plan. helpers are invited per child and per pet, and you choose exactly what each one sees from a list of toggles. a tuesday sitter who watches one child sees only that child. the document vault and your private life stay yours, and a live preview shows what they'll see first.

does it work for pets and fur-parents?

absolutely. pets are designed to get their own profiles, so you can share feeding instructions, walks, daycare pickup, medications, and vet contacts with the village who help care for them. fur-parents with demanding, unpredictable schedules get the same calm coordination — and a photo wall for the people who love them.

when does it launch?

BackPocket Mom is in pre-launch, so there's no date to share yet — we'd rather get it right than rush it. the best way to be first to know is to leave your email; we'll write to you once, the day it's ready, with the links to download it. nothing else.

how much will it cost?

pricing isn't announced yet, so we won't guess at numbers. the intent is a generous free version plus an optional paid upgrade — and your family's data is never the product, no matter what you choose. join the list and you'll be among the first to know the details once they're set.
